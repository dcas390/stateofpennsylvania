Welcome to Harrisburg, Pennsylvania!

Below you will find some information of the Capital.

Population of Harrisburg

Harrisburg has an estimated population about 50,000 people as of the most recent census.

(Source: Harrisburg Wikipedia page.)

The Year Harrisburg was incorporated

Harrisburg became incorporated in the year 1791 and then became the capital of Pennsylvania in 1812. In March 1860 "it was incorporated as the City of Harrisburg by the Senate and House of Representatives of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania."

(Source: Harrisburg Wikipedia page)

(Source: Incorporated info)

The Region Harrisburg is located

The city of Harrisburg is located in Southeastern region of Pennsylvaniaand it sits along the Susquehanna River.

(Source: Moving to Harrisburg-the Home and Money page.)

City Classification

Harrisburg is as an urban city, being a major political, cultural, and economic hub in the region. "Harrisburg is a walkable small city with great urban neighborhoods, situated on a beautiful river and geographically blessed near larger metros."

(Source: The BURG.)

Average Income Level

The average household income for Harrisburg is a bit lower than the state average. In 2023 the state of Pennsylvania had an average household income of approximately $79,820. While Harrisburg's average is closer to $46,654, this shows why the city has a more affordable housing market but also its challenges with economic inequality.

(Source: Data USA)
